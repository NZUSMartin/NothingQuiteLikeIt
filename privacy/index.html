Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Nothing Quite Like It (the "Villa"), managed by GCVC (Cayman), is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights. Where applicable, we comply with the UK GDPR, the EU GDPR, and the Cayman Islands Data Protection Act 2017.

1. Who We Are

The data controller is GCVC (Cayman), acting on behalf of the owner of Nothing Quite Like It, 134 Sand Pointe Road, Rum Point, Grand Cayman, Cayman Islands.

Contact: stay@nothingquitelikeit.com

2. What Data We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity data: full name, nationality.
  • Contact data: email address, telephone number, postal address.
  • Booking data: arrival and departure dates, number of guests, special requests, rental agreement details.
  • Financial data: payment amounts and transaction references. Note: we do not store full card numbers — payment processing is handled by our payment provider.
  • Communications data: the content of any emails, WhatsApp messages, or contact form submissions you send us.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, pages visited, and time spent on our website (see Cookies, clause 7).

3. How We Collect Your Data

We collect personal data when you:

  • Submit an enquiry or contact form on our website;
  • Register your interest via the email sign-up form;
  • Make a booking or rental enquiry by email, phone, or WhatsApp;
  • Correspond with us before, during, or after your stay;
  • Visit our website (technical data collected automatically).

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To respond to enquiries: processing and replying to your messages (legitimate interest / pre-contractual steps).
  • To fulfil your booking: administering the rental agreement, coordinating your arrival and services (contract performance).
  • To send availability and news updates: only where you have consented — for example, by registering your interest (consent).
  • To comply with legal obligations: including tax and financial record-keeping requirements under Cayman Islands law (legal obligation).
  • To protect our legitimate interests: managing security deposits, pursuing unpaid invoices, or making legal claims.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party for their marketing purposes.

5. Who We Share Data With

We share personal data only where necessary:

  • Villa management and concierge staff: to coordinate your arrival and services.
  • Service providers: such as our email platform, form submission processor (Netlify / Formspree), and payment gateway — bound by data processing agreements.
  • Professional advisers: accountants, legal advisers — under confidentiality obligations.
  • Regulators and authorities: where required by law.

We do not transfer personal data outside of your jurisdiction unless appropriate safeguards are in place (such as Standard Contractual Clauses where EU/UK GDPR applies).

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary:

  • Booking and financial records: 7 years from the date of the rental, to satisfy legal and tax obligations.
  • Enquiry data where no booking follows: 2 years from the date of the enquiry.
  • Marketing consent data: until you withdraw consent.
  • Technical/website data: typically 12 months.

7. Cookies & Analytics

Our website uses minimal, privacy-first analytics. We do not use Google Analytics or any third-party advertising cookies. We may use a privacy-respecting analytics tool (such as Plausible or Fathom) that does not collect personal data and does not require a cookie consent banner.

Essential cookies may be set by our hosting provider (Netlify) for security and performance purposes. These do not identify individuals.

You can disable cookies in your browser settings; this will not affect your ability to use our website.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: to ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure: to ask us to delete your data, subject to legal obligations.
  • Restriction: to ask us to limit how we use your data.
  • Portability: to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection: to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Withdraw consent: at any time, for processing based on your consent (e.g. marketing emails). Withdrawal does not affect prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at stay@nothingquitelikeit.com. We will respond within 30 days.

If you are located in the UK or EU and believe we have not handled your data correctly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g. the ICO in the UK, or your EU Data Protection Authority).

9. Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or destruction. Our website is served over HTTPS. Access to personal data is restricted to those who need it to carry out their responsibilities.

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. If you believe your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately.

10. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (for example, excursion providers or the Cayman Islands Tourism Authority). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and recommend you read their policies separately.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will always be published on this page with the date it was last updated. Material changes will be notified to guests with confirmed bookings by email.
